The Reopening Threshold

When contradiction must stop being absorbed and begin to govern reassessment.

This essay argues for a simple diagnostic-governance rule: when a provisional frame begins to organise records, access, interpretation, and later institutional response, the record should also state what would make that frame lose authority.

The issue is not diagnostic perfection. It is diagnostic reversibility. A record may begin in uncertainty, but it becomes unsafe when uncertainty is preserved in language while being denied operational force.

A reopening threshold is the point at which accumulated mismatch stops being treated as background complexity and begins to require pause, qualification, referral, testing, or reassessment.

The central question is therefore not only: what was the diagnosis? It is also: what would have made the system reopen the diagnosis once contrary signals accumulated?
