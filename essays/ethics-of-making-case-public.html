The Ethics of Making the Case Public

A public archive needs restraint, redaction, and a clear reading contract.

This site is not a verdict. It is a structured archive. Its purpose is to let readers examine how documents, experience, interpretation, and later reflection relate to one another.

The patient is both author and subject. That position requires care. The archive should distinguish source material from commentary, argument from finding, and unresolved question from conclusion.

Redaction is part of that discipline. It protects privacy and keeps attention on the governance question rather than on unnecessary personal detail.

The ethical value of publication lies in disciplined witness. The archive gives shape to an experience that was already shaped by records. It does so best when it remains fair, proportionate, and explicit about its limits.
