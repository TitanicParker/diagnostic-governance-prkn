Narrative medicine

Living between definitions

This lens examines the patient’s lived relationship to pain, gait, feet, diagnosis, identity, and the medical record.

A diagnostic label does not only name a condition. It gives a patient a story to live inside. When the label is incomplete, the patient may be forced to live between the named disorder and the actual disorder.

The narrative medicine route treats the body and the record as co-authors of the illness story. It asks how foot pain, dystonia, gait failure, medication response, and later genetic confirmation altered the patient’s ability to understand their own life.

Key questions

  • What happens when the record supplies a story that the body keeps contradicting?
  • How does a patient relate to their feet when the feet are both symptom and disputed evidence?
  • How does a named diagnosis shape fear, hope, identity, and planning?
  • What is lost when the actual disorder remains unnamed?
