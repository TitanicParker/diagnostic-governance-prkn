Health-data ethics

Records as active instruments of care

This lens examines accuracy, completeness, rectification, restriction, continuing reliance, and the governance of medical data.

Working thesis: a medical record is not a passive archive. It can become an active instrument that organises future care, access, and institutional response.

Health-data ethics asks whether the record remains accurate enough for the uses to which it is put. A record may be historically accurate as a note of what a clinician believed, while still becoming misleading if later relied upon without qualification.

Key questions

  • When does an incomplete record become misleading through continued reliance?
  • What does rectification mean when the issue is context and qualification rather than erasure?
  • How should disputed diagnostic data be restricted or flagged?
  • What duties arise when downstream care is shaped by a frame later shown to be incomplete?
