GDPR lens

Accuracy, access, and continuing reliance

This lens examines the case as a health-data and data-protection problem: what follows when medical data are historically recorded, later disputed, and still relied upon to shape care?

The data-protection issue is not merely whether a past note accurately recorded what a clinician once believed. It is whether continued reliance on that data, after dispute and later reframing, remains fair, accurate, and clinically safe.

In this archive, data accuracy is inseparable from care. Rectification, restriction, access, and controller responsibility become relevant because the record does not simply describe the patient. It shapes what care can be requested, authorised, resisted, or understood.

Key questions

  • When does a historically accurate record become misleading through continued reliance?
  • What does rectification require where the problem is context, qualification, and governing effect?
  • How should disputed diagnostic data be treated while correction is unresolved?
  • What duties arise where hospital-controlled records and teaching-related records may sit across different controllers?
  • What does access mean when the patient seeks to test a remembered clinical-educational event against institutional records?
