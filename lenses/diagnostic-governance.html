Diagnostic governance

From provisional frame to governing reality

This lens studies how uncertainty, labels, biomarkers, genetics, and clinical habit can harden into administrative truth.

Working thesis: the issue is not only whether a diagnosis was correct. The issue is what the diagnosis was allowed to govern before the explanatory sequence was complete.

A diagnosis can describe. It can also organise. Once it organises prescriptions, referrals, access, self-understanding, risk interpretation, and institutional response, it becomes governance.

This site uses the term diagnostic governance to describe that second function: the way a clinical frame becomes the practical reality through which the patient is processed and understood.

Key questions

  • When does a provisional hypothesis become administrative finality?
  • What is the ethical status of uncertainty that is recorded but not operationally active?
  • How should a record behave when genetic causation remains live but unresolved?
  • What duties arise when later evidence contradicts or reframes the governing label?
