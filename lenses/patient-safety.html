Patient safety

Inherited frames and downstream risk

This lens considers the safety consequences of inherited diagnostic assumptions, handover, non-reopening, and specialist authority.

Working thesis: patient safety is affected when later clinicians inherit a record that appears settled even though the original uncertainty was never fully resolved.

Diagnostic safety is not only about the first diagnosis. It is also about whether systems can reopen a frame when later information accumulates. A record that has become authoritative can make correction more difficult for every downstream actor.

Key questions

  • What signals should trigger reassessment?
  • How does specialist authority constrain primary care?
  • When does a persistent symptom become evidence of a safety problem?
  • How should systems preserve uncertainty across time?
