Limits and fairness

Limits of claim

This site is designed as an ethical and scholarly case study. It does not ask readers to make disciplinary, legal, or personal conclusions.

This site does not claim

  • that any clinician acted maliciously;
  • that the initial 2017 diagnosis was indefensible by itself;
  • that later genetic confirmation was knowable with certainty in 2017;
  • that a court, regulator, or professional body has made findings on the issues discussed here;
  • that individual blame is the correct or only interpretive frame.

This site does claim

That the record raises serious ethical and scholarly questions about how uncertainty is disclosed, how diagnostic labels govern care, how patient testimony is handled, and how medical records continue to organise later interpretation.

Core question: what happens when uncertainty is recorded but not allowed to govern?
