Ethical commitments

Redaction policy

This site treats publication as an ethical act. Redaction is used to protect dignity, privacy, third parties, and non-essential identifying information.

Redaction principles

  • Remove or obscure addresses, phone numbers, hospital numbers, PPS numbers, dates of birth, email addresses, and other direct identifiers unless the patient has made a deliberate authorial choice to disclose their own details elsewhere.
  • Remove or obscure names and identifying details of family members, non-essential clinicians, administrative staff, witnesses, and unrelated third parties.
  • Exclude clinical material that is intimate, irrelevant, or disproportionate to the ethical analysis.
  • Separate source quotation from commentary.
  • Avoid gratuitous personal criticism.
  • Do not invite harassment, contact, reputational targeting, or disciplinary conclusions against any individual.
Publication rule: no raw clinical document should be published before a separate redaction pass has been completed.
