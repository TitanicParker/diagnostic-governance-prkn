The case as a web of relationships

This section tracks the relations through which diagnostic governance operated: body, diagnosis, disorder, clinician, GP, senior authority, quality and safety, and data control.

The case is not only a patient-diagnosis relationship. It is a network of clinical, institutional, epistemic, and data-governance relationships.

Relations

  • Patient and feet: the body’s originating signal.
  • Patient and named diagnosis: idiopathic Parkinson’s disease as lived frame.
  • Patient and actual disorder: PRKN/PARK2-related monogenic Parkinsonism with dystonia.
  • Patient and neurologist: trust, explanation, asymmetry, and patient-facing correspondence.
  • Neurologist and GP: specialist authority transmitted through the record.
  • Neurologist and senior authority: treatment caution, hierarchy, and diagnostic closure.
  • Patient and Quality & Patient Safety: complaint handling and institutional interpretation.
  • Patient and data controller: accuracy, rectification, restriction, and continuing reliance.
  • Patient and future readers: the ethics of making the case public.
