Source extract 1

Neurology note: Dear [GP], It was a pleasure to see [Patient] at clinic today. The medication is as detailed above. This is largely similar. The Lyrica is 150 mg 4 times per day. Generally the Sinemet is on an empty stomach except for after dinner. He does not drink a lot of fluids specifically with the Sinemets, but he is drinking plenty during the day. As regards the liquid with Sinemet we tend to like a good volume of non-milky fluid being taken with this. Therefore a half a glass or a glass of the cordial that he likes, would be fine. The Anxicalm dose is half a tablet - he is not sure how much taken at night. If and when the Rivotril becomes available, he would prefer and I think we would prefer as well that he would go back onto his old Rivotril please in lieu of the Anxicalm. In general terms, [Patient] explains he is living within his boundaries. He’s generally well and he feels he’s functioning well within his day, but he does realise life is “a lot smaller”. E.g. it takes a long time to fold clothes - especially in the evenings - he’s very tired. Pumping tyres on the bike means that after 7 mins his toes will be curling in. He is down to darts 90 min per week down from 3 hrs per day. This is a function of the foot pain made worse by standing playing darts. The Lyrica removed the foot pain – the right foot is much better, there is still some in the left. But it isn’t relieving the toe curling in when playing darts. He is happy to walk or cycle miles. [Patient] doesn’t like wakening early in the morning (and he doesn’t like it if he wakens at night – a folded sheet will annoy him). He didn’t realise how much anxiety he had til the Lyrica. He doesn’t get moody (used get for 3-4 days as he used to have every 3-4 weeks) and he thinks it’s the Lyrica, [but also it could be from mental hygiene (and [Patient] is of course likely quite right in both respects).]. Most of the time he’s not dyskietic til after the first pint (of 4-5) at c 9PM. There was minimal dyskinesia of both hands walking but it looked close to normal. [Patient] asked me about the Fabry testing. This had been sent last summer but unfortunately they do not send us the results directly unlike radiology and therefore I had not sent this out. I do apologise. Basically Alpha galactosidase and the beta Hexosaminidase testing were all in the normal ranges for [Patient]. Therefore a Fabry disease is not the explanation for his Parkinson's. Impression. The question is whether we should change anything at the moment. Himself [Patient] would feel the medication is “spot on”. There was no dyskinesia today. Alcohol is 4-5 pints every night which he knows I feel is much too much. I’d really prefer if he was on thiamine because of this. Recommendations. I do feel that we should shift the evening c5PM Sinemet to before food with a good volume of water. (He’d doesn’t mind trying this but if its problematic he’ll go back to the way he was.) We have an option on a new COMPTESS like drug now. The problem is that he may become excessively dyskinetic? The worry is that even on relatively minimal Sinemet (and it is minimal) we can see the mild dyskinesia in his hands when we look for it. This is the classic “balancing problem” in terms of trying to help him in the evenings, versus, pushing him too far from a dopamine perspective and making him dyskinetic. I’d prefer he’d been on the Thiamine please 100mg, but a family member assists with nutrition. But I don’t want him at risk of an alcohol related neuropathy. He knows I worry about the amount of alcohol. When the Rivotril becomes available again, I’d be grateful if he could be switched back to this at night time please. Follow-up: In 1 year Neurology Clinic. I do hope this is of assistance. If you have any questions or concerns please do not hesitate to contact me. Thank you, Yours sincerely, [Consultant Neurologist]
