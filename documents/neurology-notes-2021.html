Source extract 1

Neurology Clinic – Oct 2021-10-27th Dear [GP], It was a pleasure to see [Patient] back at neurology outpatient’s clinic today. Of note, [Patient] has started doing a mediation course - which is going quite well. As regards current status, [Patient] would describe that yesterday was a particularly very good day. Going back to September 2021, [Patient] had felt a bit undertreated. He had contacted [Parkinson's Nurse] Borwne and [Patient] was given what would be viewed in fairness as a very tiny dose of Sinemet. That was 62.5 mg twice daily (12.5/50mg BD) as the first start of a Levodopa drug. In the context of doing the course, it is a bother to him going between the reading glasses and the distance glasses. Therefore he had made an appointment for varifocals with the optician. He had slightly high pressures apparently at the optician. [Patient] then contacted [Parkinson's Nurse] about that and [Parkinson's Nurse] suggested that we would remove the Kemadrin and replace it with the Sinemet (for the ocular pressure). [Patient] thinks that this might allow the eyes be somewhat better. [Patient] just had another intraocular pressure test yesterday with the optician and the pressure was still a bit raised (which was 23 and 24 where 22 would be the optimal maximum, they explained and 2 years previously the level had been 18). [Patient] is obviously hoping that the Sinemet change with the increased dose now to 4 times per day will more than compensate for the Kemadrin removal and he is tolerating the Sinemet introduction and escalate well.. The weight is fine he explains. He is not specifically dieting. To me, he looks like he has lost a fair amount of weight. He is still carrying some central adiposity, however it is not bad compared with before. In terms of temperament, [Patient] would feel relaxed and peaceful in himself. Obviously one of the biggest questions with the Sinemet was what are the impulse control disorder (ICD) elements like? (In fairness most of the impulse control problems that we see tends to be more with the synthetic agents like the neupro, and the pramipexole and the ropinirole). He describes a slight intensity may be in the first 3 or 4 days of the Sinemet starting and he had noticed a bit of change. (ICD elements can be alcohol, increased spending, surreptitious spending, feeling of lack of control, wanting to buy things, obsessional thoughts). Nevertheless that does seem to have settled but he (unlike many people with impulse control disorders) still retains very good insight into it and I think we can depend on him. That may not always be the case and [Patient] knows this. The alcohol is still gone. On the other side of the impulse control issues, a part of that can be that tobacco use and cannabis use were recorded; quantities are redacted as non-essential for publication. (most smokers who get Parkinsons seem to stop – likely for lack of dopminerigc reward reinforcement of tobacco) Impression At the current time [Patient] seems to be in a relatively good place. In many respects in life I am always telling people that we “have to live in the day”. That is obviously what is important to QoL. Nevertheless we also do have to have a responsibility to the future. He is well aware of the impulse control disorder concerns that we have with the Sinemet but the impulse control element of things seems to him to be “well under wraps”. There is also the Chorea. The other thing that I am a little bit concerned about is that effectively the Sinemet dose has become 62.5 mg x4 times per day rather quickly. Compared with other people with Parkinson’s who would be self increasing the Sinemet rather quickly, the circumstances here were somewhat justified: The Kemadrin was being removed as it seemed that it needed to go. But the thing that has to be understood here is that his effective dose of Sinemet is probably higher than other people on Sinemet 62.5 mg x4 times per day because he is actually on the Comtess already. (Conversely most Sinemet is after food) Therefore what I am trying to articulate here is that I do not want the Sinemet rising significantly above where we are. Already he has had the slightest awareness of chorea while playing darts. It is a general principle of Parkinson’s management all over the world that we try and spare Sinemet for as long as possible in people who are very young (and [Patient] is still just 44). Additionally when Sinemet is used, the aim should be to keep the dosing as absolutely minimal as possible. One issue that I have to acknowledge that [Patient] is hopeful and looking forward to is that the “cognitive effects” that can happen with Kemadrin (which I have never been really worried too much about especially in [Patient]) but which he has always been aware of may improve with the removal of the Kemadrin. I would guess that the pressure in the eyes will probably subside over the next few weeks and he can have the eye pressure checked again. If the eye pressure is still raised then obviously it is a question of a glaucomatous situation and likely then quite independent of the Kemadrin. He has already paid for the varifocal. He might as well get them because one way or another, given his normal age anyway, it is expected that he would naturally be requiring distance vision glasses along with reading glasses. Nevertheless it will be interesting to see does he actually notice anything different subjectively in vision, with the apparent reduction in pressure? [Patient] has been fully counselled on all of the above and indeed presently will have a copy of this directly at home. He mentioned about DBS today. As I have reminded him, the DBS is only ever for the tremor element. It will not help with anything else. He will likely always remain a candidate for DBS and we can look into this ad any time if he wishes to proceed with that Follow-up: 12 months Will review with ANP [Parkinson's Nurse] in interim if issues. I do hope this is of assistance. If you have any questions or concerns please do not hesitate to contact me. Thank you, Yours sincerely, [Consultant Neurologist]
