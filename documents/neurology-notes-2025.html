Source extract 1

* Neurology note: Dear [GP], It was a pleasure to see [Patient] at clinic today. The medication is as detailed above. The major change is a reduction in the Lyrica from the maximum 600mg/day down to a total of 50mg 3 times per day. Thiamine is not really being taken anymore but that is less likely to matter because all alcohol finished on the 07/August/2024. The one thing that is on [Patient]'s mind is foot pain. I have gone through the other Parkinsons first. The complete cessation of 4 or 5 pints of Guinness each day was associated with sudden and massive constipation. This is why he is doing the AllBran. It is absolutely necessary to him. If he didn't like the AllBran, we would suggest Movicol but he is happy with this and he would really prefer to avoid Movicol. Wiggling dyskinesia is something he doesn’t notice hugely, but he sees a household pet appeared to notice the left foot movements. In actual fact, what he is describing in the afternoon in terms of the dyskinesia is a dyskinetic walk. That spread of the feet and feeling of movement of the feet and how he mimics, this is more consistent with dyskinesia. In the examination of his walk, the dyskinesia does become mildly more prominent. He tends to hold his hands together which is probably subconscious and traps them to a degree hiding the dyskinesia and appearing to normalise the appearance of the walk People don’t comment on it though. Sleeping is excellent. There is no dreaming remembered. There isn’t much tremor. He isn’t doing the darts anymore As he observes, being seen in the morning time in clinic is not ideal because the morning time is quite simply the best time of the day for [Patient]. He does feel "more cornered" by the Parkinsons condition especially in the evening times. The feet (but mainly the left): Since 2005 he simple describes “my feet disturb me”. He hobbles out of bed. He takes the meds but the feet are too sore to tie the runners. Each morning it feels like “murder in the feet”, and there is discomfort. The first 150 footsteps are torture. He feels he’s festinating but from the pain. 500-600 steps are done in Wellies over rocks on the beach. To say “I won’t live with it” allows him put words on the situation. As regards the reduction in the Lyrica, [GP] had talked about reducing this. [Patient] wasn’t sure why. But he reduced the Lyrica himself 6 months after [GP] left it where it was (probably from August September 2024 when he start the Healthy Life approach himself). Himself [Patient] had thought that the improvements that he had psychologically were from improvements in mental hygiene. As we acknowledged last year that most certainly could have been a factor in the anxiety element improvement. Because of those improvements, [Patient] had reduced the Lyrica. Once the Parkinsons medications hit his system he does have a relief in the feelings in the feet. Impression. First of all, it is an absolutely excellent result that the alcohol is completely gone. If there is a degree of peripheral neuropathy here, the alcohol being gone will hopefully slow the deterioration of that. Just so we are clear, I have gone through my own letter of last year with [Patient]. I had not actually suggested or otherwise implied that the Lyrica be reduced. Most certainly it was noted that he was at the maximum dose of this in 2024, at 150mg 4 times per day equivalent to 600mg. I suspect that he is psychologically much better as a function of all of the mental hygiene work he has done and continues to practice. I do not think he really needs the Lyrica from the perspective of the psychology. He knows very well that once the Sinemet hits his system that the feet feel better from a pain perspective. That is commonly described. Sinemet should not do anything for pain but it will relieve the experience of pain to at least some degree. For example if he had had old serious ankle or leg fractures that were still playing up Sinemet would probably make them feel better as well. However, I do think he is missing the Lyrica from the perspective of the feet? Quite simply, pain is not really a direct part of Parkinson's at all. Certainly if person has dystonia a person can have painful dystonia. However, the type of pain that he is describing is not a direct Parkinson's phenomenology. But I have little doubt other than that if we increased the Sinemet, he would probably get some further relief from the pain. It would not likely go away. But there would be some relief. But the problem is that if we increase his Sinemet, then he will get much more severe dyskinesias. Therefore, increasing "Parkinson's" medications will only be of limited help and probably associated with significant complications. I do not mind trying if [Patient] would like to try that. But an avoidance of a dose increase of Parkinsons meds is why I would suggest a consideration somewhat increasing the Lyrica. His own feeling in this regard would be, that he feels that perhaps an element of this is how bad the Parkinson's is expected to be in the morning times - because he has not had medications for example overnight. In this regard, there would be the option on giving him a slow release Sinemet at night time? The last Sinemet in the evening - night time at the current time is taken at about 8 PM, and bed is at 1 AM and he wakens to get up at 0845 hrs. As he explains he would be definitely open to trying this. I cannot promise [Patient] that this is going to work. Also it may be associated with intolerable dyskinesias.Furthermore it may promote night-time dreaming. He knows all of this from before - but he is willing to try and I do think it is very reasonable to try. The very first thing that he would be interested in trying - and it is entirely reasonable might be to try to wake up around about 08:00am and swallows the tablets while still in bed. I didn't suggest this myself because we do not like breaking people's sleep, but in reality it is what a lot of patients will do to "get themselves going". He actually doesn't mind trying this at all because he will have woken and gone to the toilet probably before 8 anyway. First of all, we will start Sinemet controlled release for [Patient]. He is going to take this probably around somewhere between 12 midnight and 1AM - probably as close to going to bed as is convenient - maybe around the time he takes his Anxicalm. The will be a half tablet of a “Half Sinemet CR”. This will be for about 2 months. Assuming he is tolerating that without difficulty then it can be moved to a whole tablet of a “Half Sinemet CR” taken at night time. He was wondering about seeing a chronic pain specialist. It would be my own suspicion that they would probably recommend Amitriptyline or increasing the Lyrica. As he explains himself, it would be his view that at the 600mg of the Lyrica the pain was certainly not eliminated. Nevertheless, the concern is that he was doing relatively better on that then now. Follow-up: In 1 year Neurology Clinic. I do hope this is of assistance. If you have any questions or concerns please do not hesitate to contact me. Thank you, Yours sincerely, [Consultant Neurologist]
